# Analysis-on-informal-employment-and-Poverty
# Informal Employment, Poverty and Social Protection in Côte d’Ivoire

This repository contains a set of analytical outputs and visualizations produced as part of a doctoral research essay examining the relationship between **informal employment, multidimensional poverty, and health insurance coverage in Côte d’Ivoire**.

The objective is to translate econometric results into **clear, intuitive, and policy-relevant visuals** that can be understood by both technical and non-technical audiences in academic seminars and supervisory discussions.

---

###  `map_poverty_multidimentional.emf`
Spatial distribution of the **Multidimensional Poverty Index (MPI)** across regions of Côte d’Ivoire.

- Type: Choropleth / heat map  
- Level: Regional analysis  
- Purpose: Highlight territorial disparities in poverty  

**Key insight:** Poverty is highly heterogeneous across regions, revealing strong spatial inequalities.

### `marginal_effect.emf`
Marginal effects of **informal employment on multidimensional poverty**, moderated by **Universal Health Coverage (UHC)**.

- Type: Interaction / marginal effects plot  
- Focus: Informal employment × health insurance coverage  

**Key insight:** Health insurance coverage reduces the adverse effect of informal employment on poverty, indicating a protective role of social protection mechanisms.

---

### `Informal_employment_determinants.png`
Determinants of **employment formalization levels** in Côte d’Ivoire.

- Type: Coefficient / predicted effects plot  
- Model: Econometric model of employment formality  

**Key insight:** Education, sector of activity, and socio-demographic characteristics significantly influence the probability of formal employment.

---

## 🛠️ Methodology

- Software: Stata,  
- Methods:
  - Econometric modeling (regression and interaction effects)
  - Marginal effects estimation
  - Spatial data visualization
  - Simplified presentation of predicted outcomes

## 🎯 Main Message

Informal workers in Côte d’Ivoire are more exposed to multidimensional poverty, but **social protection mechanisms such as health insurance coverage can significantly mitigate this vulnerability**.
